# Standard Operating Procedure: Lead Qualification

## Purpose
This SOP defines the process for qualifying inbound leads to ensure we focus resources on high-potential prospects.

## Scope
Applies to all inbound leads from website forms, emails, phone calls, and referrals.

## Qualification Criteria

### BANT Framework
We use the BANT framework to qualify leads:

#### Budget
- **Qualified**: Has budget within our pricing range ($299-$2,499+/month)
- **Partially Qualified**: Budget not yet confirmed but company size indicates ability to pay
- **Unqualified**: Budget significantly below our minimum ($299/month)

#### Authority
- **Qualified**: Decision maker or direct influencer
- **Partially Qualified**: Can connect us with decision maker
- **Unqualified**: No influence on purchasing decision

#### Need
- **Qualified**: Clear pain point that our solution addresses
- **Partially Qualified**: General interest but needs further discovery
- **Unqualified**: No clear need or out of scope

#### Timeline
- **Qualified**: Ready to purchase within 3 months
- **Partially Qualified**: Exploring options, 3-6 month timeline
- **Unqualified**: No timeline or >6 months

## Qualification Scoring

Calculate qualification score based on BANT:
- Budget: 0-30 points
- Authority: 0-25 points
- Need: 0-30 points
- Timeline: 0-15 points

**Total Score: 0-100**

### Score Interpretation:
- **70-100**: Highly qualified - Priority follow-up
- **50-69**: Qualified - Standard follow-up
- **30-49**: Marginally qualified - Nurture campaign
- **0-29**: Unqualified - Politely decline or nurture long-term

## Qualification Questions

### Initial Contact (First 5 minutes)
1. "What challenges are you currently facing?"
2. "How are you handling [specific problem] today?"
3. "What's driving you to look for a solution now?"
4. "Who else is involved in this decision?"
5. "What's your timeline for making a decision?"

### Budget Discovery (If qualified on need)
1. "Have you allocated budget for this initiative?"
2. "What's the cost of not solving this problem?"
3. "Are you familiar with our pricing?" (share pricing page)

### Authority Confirmation
1. "Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"
2. "What's your typical procurement process?"
3. "Would it be helpful to include [decision maker] in our next conversation?"

## Next Steps by Qualification Level

### Highly Qualified (70-100)
1. Schedule demo/discovery call within 24 hours
2. Assign to senior sales rep
3. Send personalized proposal within 3 days
4. Follow up every 2-3 days until closed

### Qualified (50-69)
1. Schedule demo within 1 week
2. Assign to sales rep
3. Send standard proposal
4. Follow up weekly

### Marginally Qualified (30-49)
1. Add to nurture email campaign
2. Send relevant content (case studies, guides)
3. Check in monthly
4. Re-qualify in 3 months

### Unqualified (0-29)
1. Send polite email thanking them for interest
2. Provide helpful resources (blog posts, free tools)
3. Add to newsletter list
4. Leave door open for future engagement

## Red Flags

Immediate disqualification for:
- Competitor intelligence gathering
- Student projects (unless educational plan)
- Inappropriate or abusive communication
- Clear mismatch with our solution
- Requesting spec work or free consulting

## Escalation

Escalate to sales manager if:
- Enterprise deal (>$50k annual value)
- Strategic account
- Complex requirements
- Legal or compliance questions
- Pricing negotiation outside standard bounds

## Documentation

Record in CRM:
- Qualification score with breakdown
- Notes from qualification questions
- Next steps and timeline
- Any special requirements or concerns

## Success Metrics

Track monthly:
- Lead volume by source
- Qualification rate (% qualified)
- Speed to qualify (average hours)
- Qualified lead to customer conversion rate
- Revenue from qualified leads
